ABOUT ELECTRIC GUITARS














Types of Electric Guitars

  1. Solidbody

    2. These are the most common type (like the Fender Stratocaster). Because they have no hollow sound chamber, they can be played at extremely high volumes without feedback, making them perfect for Rock and Metal.

  2. Semi-Hollow Body

    3. These feature a solid block of wood running down the center, but the wings are hollow. This clever design gives you the warmth of a hollow body but the feedback resistance of a solid body (famous in Blues and Indie rock).

  3. Hollowbody

    4. Often called "Jazz Boxes," these are fully hollow like an acoustic guitar. They produce a warm, resonant tone perfect for Jazz, but they tend to screech with feedback if you use too much distortion.

  4. Types of Pickups
    • Single Coil: A single coil of copper wire around a plastic bobbin with several magnetic pole pieces. Known for a bright, "glassy," and crisp sound. Famous users include Jimi Hendrix and David Gilmour.
    • Humbucker: Two coils of copper wire around two seperate bobbins wound in opposite directions with magnetic pole pieces of opposite polarities. Two coils wired together to cancel out background noise ("buck the hum"). They sound thicker, warmer, and louder.

  5. Extended Range Guitars

    6. While standard guitars have 6 strings, heavy metal genres (like Djent) often utilize 7, 8, or even 9-string guitars to hit ultra-low bass notes.

How Electric Guitars Work

Signal Chain: Pickups - Output Jack - Cable - Amplifier

  • When the strings vibrate over the pickups they generate an electrical current.
  • The current travels through a cable to the amplifier where the signal is amplified and altered to the players settings.
